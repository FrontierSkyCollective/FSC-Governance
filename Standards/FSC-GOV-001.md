FSC-GOV-001
Governance and Decision-Making Standard
Draft 0.1
Purpose
This standard establishes how Frontier Sky Collective is organized, how decisions are made, and how organizational records are maintained.
The objective of this standard is to ensure continuity, accountability, transparency, and institutional memory as the organization grows.

Governance Principles
The Collective shall operate according to the following principles:
Evidence-based decision making
Transparency of process
Documentation of decisions
Accountability of responsibilities
Preservation of institutional knowledge

Organizational Structure
The Collective may establish divisions, programs, committees, and working groups as necessary.
Initial divisions may include:
Research Division
Observatory Division
Technology Division
Publications Division

Decision Categories
Operational Decisions
Routine decisions concerning day-to-day operations.
Examples:
Software selection
Hardware purchases
Repository structure
Approval:
Responsible project owner.

Program Decisions
Decisions affecting research programs.
Examples:
New observatory deployment
New research initiative
Significant methodology changes
Approval:
Governance review.

Strategic Decisions
Decisions affecting organizational direction.
Examples:
Charter revisions
Partnerships
Funding models
Organizational restructuring
Approval:
Formal governance vote.

Decision Documentation
All significant decisions shall be documented.
Minimum requirements:
Decision ID
Date
Participants
Rationale
Outcome
Format:
FSC-DEC-XXXX
Example:
FSC-DEC-0001

Meetings
Meeting records shall include:
Agenda
Attendance
Decisions
Action items
Meeting IDs:
FSC-M001
 FSC-M002
etc.

Version Control
All governance documents shall be maintained through version control.
Git shall be considered the system of record.
