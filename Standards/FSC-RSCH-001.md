FSC-RSCH-001
Research Methodology Standard
Draft 0.1
Purpose
This standard defines how investigations are conducted, documented, reviewed, and published.
The objective is to ensure consistency, reproducibility, and scientific rigor.

Core Principle
Observations drive hypotheses.
Hypotheses shall not drive the collection or interpretation of evidence.

Investigation Lifecycle
Phase 1 – Observation
Collection of:
Sensor data
Images
Video
Environmental data
Witness information (if applicable)

Phase 2 – Preservation
Original data shall be preserved whenever possible.
Raw data shall remain unchanged.
Derived products shall be clearly identified.

Phase 3 – Analysis
Investigators shall:
Review observations
Identify known explanations
Compare available evidence
Document assumptions

Phase 4 – Classification
Proposed categories:
Identified
Explanation supported by available evidence.
Probable
Strong evidence but incomplete certainty.
Inconclusive
Insufficient evidence for determination.
Unknown
Evidence exists but no explanation currently supported.

Phase 5 – Publication
Research findings should include:
Data sources
Methodology
Findings
Limitations
Confidence assessment

Research Integrity Requirements
Investigators shall:
Distinguish fact from interpretation
Disclose uncertainty
Preserve contradictory evidence
Avoid sensational conclusions

Peer Review
Future objective:
Establish internal review process prior to publication.
