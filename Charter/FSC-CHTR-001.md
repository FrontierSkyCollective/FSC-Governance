Frontier Sky Collective
Research Charter
Purpose
Frontier Sky Collective is an independent research collective dedicated to the systematic observation, documentation, analysis, and investigation of anomalous and poorly understood phenomena.
The Collective exists to advance understanding through rigorous inquiry, empirical observation, interdisciplinary collaboration, and the responsible application of scientific methods and emerging technologies.
Frontier Sky Collective maintains no institutional commitment to any predetermined explanation, hypothesis, or conclusion. Findings are derived from available evidence and remain subject to revision as new information becomes available.

Mission
To produce credible observations, analyses, datasets, methodologies, and publications that contribute to the understanding of anomalous phenomena and related scientific questions.  Frontier Sky Collective may develop observational platforms, software systems, data standards, and research methodologies necessary to support its mission. 
Vision
To establish a respected and enduring research organization recognized for methodological rigor, intellectual integrity, and meaningful contributions to the study of anomalous phenomena.
A collaborative and evidence-driven research organization capable of developing observational infrastructure, generating high-quality datasets, and advancing understanding of anomalous and poorly understood phenomena.


Foundational Principles
Evidence as the Primary Standard
All observations, analyses, and conclusions shall be evaluated according to the quality, reliability, and availability of supporting evidence.
Assertions unsupported by evidence shall not be treated as established findings.
Methodological Neutrality
Investigations shall not begin with predetermined conclusions.
Hypotheses shall be developed, tested, refined, or rejected according to available evidence.
The Collective neither advocates for nor dismisses any explanation without sufficient evidentiary support.
Intellectual Integrity
The Collective acknowledges uncertainty where uncertainty exists.
Distinctions shall be maintained between observation, interpretation, hypothesis, and conclusion.
Members are expected to revise positions when warranted by new evidence.
Transparency and Reproducibility
Research methods, analytical processes, and supporting data should be documented to the greatest extent practical.
Findings should be reproducible, independently reviewable, and open to constructive criticism.
Where practical, Frontier Sky Collective shall promote reproducibility, transparency, and open standards to enable independent verification, collaboration, and future participation. 


Interdisciplinary Collaboration
The investigation of anomalous phenomena frequently requires expertise spanning multiple scientific and technical disciplines.
The Collective encourages collaboration across fields including astronomy, atmospheric science, engineering, data science, artificial intelligence, remote sensing, radio science, and related domains.
Standards and Documentation
Frontier Sky Collective shall strive to document its methodologies, standards, procedures, and decisions in order to promote consistency, accountability, and preservation of institutional knowledge.

Areas of Research
The Collective focuses on phenomena that may be observed, measured, recorded, analyzed, or otherwise investigated through empirical methods.
Research interests may include:
Unidentified aerial phenomena
Astronomical observations
Atmospheric and environmental anomalies
Radio-frequency phenomena
Sensor and instrumentation development
Artificial intelligence-assisted analysis
Technosignature and SETI-adjacent investigations
Emerging scientific questions involving poorly understood observations
Research priorities may evolve as new technologies, methodologies, and opportunities emerge.

Standards of Investigation
Research conducted under the auspices of Frontier Sky Collective should:
Preserve original observational data whenever possible.
Maintain documented chains of evidence.
Clearly distinguish verified findings from interpretation.
Consider conventional explanations before proposing novel explanations.
Encourage independent review and verification.
Communicate limitations, uncertainties, and assumptions openly.

Organizational Conduct
The Collective rejects:
Deliberate misinformation.
Misrepresentation of evidence.
Selective use of data to support predetermined conclusions.
Sensationalism that compromises analytical integrity.
Personal attacks, harassment, or exclusionary conduct.
Claims presented as established fact without adequate evidentiary support.

Measures of Success
Success shall be measured by the quality and integrity of the investigative process and the value of resulting contributions.
Meaningful outcomes include:
Reliable observational datasets.
Development of research methodologies and instrumentation.
Publication of analytical findings.
Resolution of previously unexplained observations.
Elimination of unsupported hypotheses.
Advancement of public and scientific understanding.
Identification of genuinely novel phenomena.
A well-supported explanation and a well-supported unresolved finding are equally valuable contributions to knowledge.

Long-Term Objective
Frontier Sky Collective seeks to develop a durable research institution capable of conducting independent investigations, supporting collaborative scientific inquiry, producing credible publications, and contributing meaningfully to humanity's understanding of anomalous and poorly understood phenomena.
The Collective is committed to pursuing knowledge through disciplined investigation, intellectual honesty, and respect for evidence.
For the purposes of the Collective, an anomalous phenomenon is an observation, event, or dataset that is not immediately explained by currently available information and warrants further investigation. An anomaly should not be interpreted as evidence of any specific explanation until supported by evidence.


Guiding Statement
Evidence over assumption.
Inquiry over ideology.
Understanding over speculation.
